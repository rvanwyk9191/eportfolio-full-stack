Introduction to myself

I am a Java API focused developer with expertise in microservices, SaaS, and Event Driven architectures. I joined JPMorgan Chase after college and spent nearly six years working on various projects relating to regulation, marketing, and evergreen. After working on my last project with JPMorgan Chase, I decided to try living in Miami for six months to emerse myself in a different experience, but due to some external factors, I decided to move back to Chicago. To continue the emersion process, I joined Fiserv as a Contractor through KForce. In July 2020, I was offered a full-time position, and I currently am employed by Fiserv.

Early part of my life:

I was born in Evander, South Africa in 1991. My parents, wanting a better future for their family, decided to move to Chicago in 1999. I grew up in Aurora, Illinois and graduated from Oswego East High School, and later Lewis University in Romeoville, Illinois with my Bachelors of Science in Computer Information Systems.

What are my hobbies:

I've always been a fan of Electronic music since I was 7. I grew up being an outsider and listening to Trance music through middle and high school. In college, I bought myself DJ equipment and have stuck with DJing for the past seven years.

I was also a Cross-Country and Track runner in High School and part of college, so I keep myself active currently. I am getting more into the science of our bodies and learning everyday about what foods and exercises work best for my body. Fitness and nutrition has become a passion of mine in the last few years.

Where do I see myself in five years:

Growing up, my mom always told me to build a skill and find my passion to apply the skill to. I'm in the never ending process of growing my IT skills, but I am at least finding my passion, which is to connect to people. I would like to begin working on projects that reflect who I am as a person, where I can grow the most, and lend my passion to others in the same position. Once I get myself in the right project, I would like to move into management and focus on people development.
